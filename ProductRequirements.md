# PRODUCT REQUIREMENTS DOCUMENT

## Project Codename: *Sovereign Ink*

---

# 1. Product Vision

## 1.1 Mission

Build an autonomous system capable of generating full-length (100k–150k words), publish-ready historical-political novels set between 1700–1900 in US and European contexts, with:

* High dramatic intensity
* Political maneuvering
* Emotional stakes
* Real historical anchors
* Fictionalized dialogue and secret subplots
* Strong structural coherence
* Clean, satisfying endings

The system must operate without human co-authoring.

---

# 2. Product Philosophy

This product does NOT aim for academic reconstruction.

It aims for:

> “What it might have felt like inside the rooms of power.”

Guiding principles:

* Drama > documentation
* Character conflict > archival fidelity
* Political tension > historical footnotes
* Emotional realism > perfect chronology

However:

* Major events must remain recognizable.
* Institutions must behave plausibly.
* The era must feel authentic.

---

# 3. Scope Definition

## 3.1 In Scope

* 1700–1900 US & Europe
* Major political events (revolutions, wars, treaties, cabinet crises)
* Real historical figures allowed
* Fictional aides, diplomats, spies allowed
* Secret negotiations and dramatized subplots allowed
* Multi-POV narratives (1–4 POVs)
* 3-act or 4-act structure
* 100k–150k word novels
* Multi-pass revision pipeline

## 3.2 Out of Scope (Initial Versions)

* Fully alternate timelines
* Non-political genres
* Modern politics
* Interactive fiction
* Historical footnotes or academic apparatus
* Perfect archival accuracy

---

# 4. Core Capabilities

---

# 4.1 Historical Anchor Framework

Purpose: Ground the narrative in recognizable historical reality.

### Requirements:

The system must:

* Select a defined historical window (e.g., 1802–1804)
* Define known macro-outcomes (e.g., Louisiana Purchase occurs)
* Identify:

  * Key figures
  * Key institutions
  * Key political stakes
* Preserve:

  * Major outcomes
  * Major geopolitical consequences

Flexibility allowed in:

* Private motivations
* Secret meetings
* Interpersonal rivalries
* Emotional arcs

---

# 4.2 Institutional Plausibility Model

Purpose: Ensure political behavior feels credible.

The system must:

* Model power structures (cabinet, monarchy, parliament, military)
* Understand:

  * Authority limits
  * Factional pressures
  * Public legitimacy concerns
* Enforce plausibility without requiring strict historical fidelity

The system must reject:

* Cartoonish political behavior
* Implausible unilateral actions
* Modern ideological framing
* Unrealistic institutional speed

---

# 4.3 Dramatic Narrative Architecture

Purpose: Ensure the novel reads like compelling fiction.

Every novel must include:

* Inciting political disruption
* Escalation of stakes
* Midpoint power shift or revelation
* Moral or strategic crisis
* High-stakes climax
* Aftermath with consequences

Each scene must include:

* A clear POV
* A goal
* Opposition
* A turn
* Consequences (political or personal)

No scene may exist solely for exposition.

---

# 4.4 Character Depth Engine

Purpose: Prevent “Wikipedia with dialogue.”

For each major character, the system must define:

* Political objective
* Personal fear
* Hidden motivation
* Moral conflict
* Relationship tensions
* Emotional arc resolution

Real historical figures must:

* Feel human
* Experience doubt
* Show ambition, fear, pride, insecurity
* Have dramatized internal conflict

Fictional characters must:

* Intersect meaningfully with power structures
* Not distort macro-history

---

# 4.5 Era Tone & Language Control

Purpose: Maintain immersive authenticity.

The system must:

* Avoid modern slang
* Avoid modern psychology jargon
* Avoid corporate metaphors
* Maintain rhetorical cadence appropriate to era

Dialogue must:

* Feel formal but emotionally charged
* Reflect class and education differences
* Avoid modern idiomatic patterns

---

# 4.6 Continuity & State Management

Purpose: Prevent narrative breakdown.

The system must track:

* Timeline progression
* Political capital shifts
* Character knowledge states
* Relationships
* Institutional posture
* Open political threads
* Military or economic consequences

The system must detect:

* Broken causal chains
* Unresolved major threads
* Sudden personality reversals
* Political contradictions

---

# 4.7 Escalation & Stakes Model

Purpose: Prevent mid-book sag.

The novel must show:

* Increasing political risk
* Increasing personal risk
* Increasing reputational cost
* Increasing institutional instability

Each act must show a measurable escalation in stakes.

The climax must:

* Resolve political conflict
* Resolve character moral arc
* Produce irreversible consequences

---

# 4.8 Multi-Pass Revision Framework

The system must generate the novel in stages:

1. Structural draft (plot-focused)
2. Escalation tightening pass
3. Emotional depth pass
4. Dialogue sharpening pass
5. Repetition reduction pass
6. Thematic reinforcement pass
7. Ending pressure test

No manuscript may be finalized without all passes.

---

# 5. Quality Standards

---

## 5.1 Structural Integrity

* No dropped primary threads
* No rushed final 10%
* Clear climax
* Clean denouement

---

## 5.2 Dramatic Intensity

* No 3+ consecutive low-conflict scenes
* Regular reversals
* Strong chapter endings
* Emotional confrontation in each act

---

## 5.3 Plausibility Threshold

* Institutional behavior must feel believable
* Decisions must follow political logic
* Military actions must respect era constraints
* No modern ideological framing

---

## 5.4 Prose Quality

* Varied sentence rhythm
* Minimal repetition
* Distinct character voices
* Dialogue subtext over exposition
* No summary-heavy chapters
